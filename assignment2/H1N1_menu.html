What is H1N1

The H1N1 flu, commonly known as swine flu, is primarily caused by the H1N1 strain of the flu (influenza) virus. H1N1 is a type of influenza A virus, and H1N1 is one of several flu virus strains that can cause the seasonal flu. Symptoms of the H1N1 flu are the same as those of the seasonal flu.



H1N1 History

2009-2010

From March to April 2009, more than 1,000 people were infected with H1N1 in Mexico and the southwestern US, resulting in 106 deaths

15/05/2009, Malaysia found its first confirmed case of Influenza A (H1N1)

17/09/2009, the United States announced that it would donate pandemic influenza vaccines with 8 countries.

In December, the number of confirmed cases in various countries decreased.




Virus Transmission Route

H1N1 influenza occurs in humans, birds and pigs. The virus can be transmitted through droplets and contact.

People who are prone to serious illness: young people aged 20 to 45






Prevention & treatment

Reduce the chance of infection by vaccinating and reducing contact with animals Take four FDA-approved antiviral drugs to reduce the severity of symptoms and the risk of complications.
